\proclaim{From Folland's book \# 2.5 pages 68-69. 
Do problems  46, 47, 48,  49, 51 } 
\endproclaim 
{\bf Note}:  Problems 46 and 47 are similar to two of the counterexamples we
discussed in class to the failure of Fubini-Tonelli when some of the hypothesis are not
complied with.  
\vskip .1in 

{\bf Extra Problems }.                                                                                                                                                               
{\bf {(E.I)}}  \, Let $X=[0,1]$ endowed with the Lebesgue measure and let
$f(x,y)=  \frac{x^2-y^2}{(x^2+y^2)^2}$ if  $(x,y) \not= (0,0)$ and
$f(0,0)=0$.
\vskip .2in
 
 
(a) Use the trigonometric substitution $x = a \, \tan {\theta}$ to
prove that
$$ \int \frac{ x^2 - a^2 }{ ( x^2 + a^2 )^2 } \, dx \, = \, \frac{ -\,
x }{ x^2 + a^2 }. $$
(Hint. Recall the identities : $\sin^2 \theta = \frac{1}{2}( 1-
\cos(2 \theta) ) $; \,  $\cos^2 \theta = \frac{1}{2}( 1 +
\cos(2 \theta) ) $ and  $ \sin ( 2 \theta ) = 2 \cos\theta \, \sin
\theta $ )
 
\vskip .15in
                                                                                                                                                                                                     
(b) Use (a) to compute the integrals
$$ \int_0^1 \left[ \int_0^1 f(x,y) dm(x) \right] dm(y) $$
and
$$ \int_0^1 \left[ \int_0^1 f(x,y) dm(y) \right] dm(x) $$
and show that they are unequal.
\vskip .1in
(c) In the example of (b) which hypotheses of Fubini-Tonelli Theorem
are
violated?

\vskip .2in 

{\bf {(E.II)}} Let $ X \times Y = [0, 1] \times [1 , \infty)$
equipped with the product Lebesgue measure. Define $f: X \times Y \to
\IR$ to be the function
$$ f(x, y ) = e^{-x y} - 2 e^{ -2 xy} $$
(i) Prove that
$$ \int_0^1 [ \int_1^{\infty} f(x, y) d y ] dx \neq  \int_1^{\infty} [
\int_0^{1} f(x, y) d x ] dy $$
 
(ii) Which hypothesis of Fubini's theorem $f$ does not satisfy?
Justify your answer by proving your claim rigorously.
 
\vskip .2in
 
{\bf {(E.III)}} Let $ X \times Y = [0, \infty) \times [0 , \infty)$
equipped with the product Lebesgue measure. Define $f: X \times Y \to
\IR$ to be the function
$$ f(x, y ) =  y. e^{ - ( 1 + x^2) y^2} $$
(i) Prove that
$$ \int_0^\infty [ \int_o^{\infty} f(x, y) d x ] dy =  \int_0^{\infty}
[ \int_0^{\infty} f(x, y) d y ] dx $$
 
(ii) Use (i) to deduce that $ \int_0^{\infty} e^{-x^2} dx =
\dfrac{\sqrt{\pi}}{2} $
  
\vskip .2in
