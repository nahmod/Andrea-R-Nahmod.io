         Description: The main purpose of this course is the analysis of solutions to nonlinear dispersive equations. Dispersive PDE model  wave propagation in nature, dispersive waves are those that spread in space as time evolves while conserving energy (for example). One of the most well known equations within this class is the nonlinear Schr√∂dinger equation (NLS). 
    In the first part we develop the analytic tools needed to understand the time dynamics of solutions to the Cauchy
    initial value problem for NLS in subcritical regimes. The second part will be devoted to understanding
    the global in time dynamics and scattering theory for the defocusing NLS in the energy critical regime case.
    The latter will involve understanding profile decompositions, suitable monotonicity formulae and long-time dispersive estimates.
    As time permits, we will discuss similar results for other nonlinear dispersive and wave equations.
